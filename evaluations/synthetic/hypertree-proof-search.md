# Hypertree Proof Search Data

**Category:** Synthetic & Generated Corpora
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11491

## Description

Proof search traces generated by the Hypertree Proof Search (HyperTree) system, which applies expert iteration over formal libraries (Lean, Metamath). The dataset captures the complete proof search process — not just successful proofs but also failed attempts and intermediate states.

## Justification

### Data Diversity (7/10)
Traces are generated from exploration of formal libraries, covering a broad range of theorem types. The diversity is enhanced by the search process itself, which explores many proof paths and subgoal types beyond what a human would manually record.

### Size (6/10)
Moderate — the dataset size reflects the compute budget for the expert iteration runs. Substantial enough to be useful for training, but not at the scale of a full library extraction.

### Level of Formalization (8/10)
Successful proof traces are formally verified. Failed attempts are also captured, providing negative examples. The formalization guarantee applies to the positive data; negative examples are unverified by construction.

### Ease of Use (5/10)
The data format (proof search trees) is more complex than standard theorem-proof pairs. Requires familiarity with the HyperTree system and tree-structured data handling. Less tooling support than LeanDojo-based datasets.

### Complexity (7/10)
Targets Mathlib-level and Metamath-level theorems, which include graduate-level mathematics. The search traces capture difficult proof steps and backtracking over hard problems, making the complexity meaningfully higher than curated competition benchmarks.
