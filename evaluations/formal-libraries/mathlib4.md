# Mathlib4

**Category:** Formal Libraries
**Link:** https://github.com/leanprover-community/mathlib4

## Description

The canonical community mathematics library for Lean 4. Covers algebra, analysis, topology, number theory, category theory, and more. The de facto standard corpus for formal math AI research, actively maintained by hundreds of contributors.

## Justification

### Data Diversity (9/10)
Spans an exceptionally broad range of mathematics — undergraduate through graduate-level — across algebra, real and complex analysis, topology, number theory, combinatorics, category theory, and probability. The only reason it falls short of 10 is that some advanced research-frontier areas (e.g. geometric Langlands, homotopy theory) remain thin.

### Size (9/10)
Contains well over 150,000 theorems and lemmas as of 2025, making it by far the largest single Lean 4 corpus. Growth is continuous and substantial year-over-year.

### Level of Formalization (10/10)
Every theorem has been fully verified by the Lean 4 kernel. No proof goes unchecked; correctness is guaranteed by construction.

### Ease of Use (7/10)
The LeanDojo toolkit provides structured extraction of theorems, premises, and tactic traces. However, setting up a full Lean 4 environment and understanding the library's conventions requires meaningful investment. Improving but still nontrivial for newcomers.

### Complexity (8/10)
Ranges from undergraduate to advanced graduate mathematics. Many results are non-trivial and require deep domain knowledge. Does not skew toward competition-style problems, so extreme olympiad difficulty is less represented.
